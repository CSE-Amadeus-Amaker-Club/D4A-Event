1. Overview

This page is your practical guide for the D4A contest. It covers the essential steps to build a functional bot using the elements you've been provided :)

Goal: build a remote controlled bot that be the last having is balloon intact at the end of the match.
UniHiker K10 ESP32-S3 Wi-Fi Control Servo / Motor Outputs Browser UI

2. Bill of Materials

Hardware

  • 1 × set of lego pieces with wheels tracks, plates...
  • 1 × set of additional lego technical pieces : gears & differential
  • 2 × green servo motors (continuous rotation) : typical usage is for propulsion.
  • 2 × grey servo motors (positional 0 to 270 degree ) : typical usage is arms, direction...

    • Electronics

    • 1 × a microcontroller with Wi-Fi capability, and contest firmware
    • 1 × an extension board and its battery
    • 1 × USB-C cable

    4. Mechanical Assembly

    1. Mount the drive system first: left drive, right drive, then caster/support.
    2. Keep the battery mass low and near the center for better stability.
    3. Mount the UniHiker K10 where the screen and buttons remain accessible.
    4. Route cables away from wheels, gears, and moving horns.
    5. Leave enough slack for steering or pan/tilt movement.
    6. Add strain relief near every connector that may be tugged in motion.
    Area Good Practice Avoid
    Battery placement Centered and low High rear-heavy mounting
    K10 placement Visible and reachable Buried under wiring
    Cable routing Bundled and fixed Loose wires near wheels

    5. Wiring

    Before applying power, verify every connection one-by-one.

    1. Connect logic power according to your controller and driver requirements.
    2. Connect left and right drive outputs with labels so you can swap quickly if direction is reversed.
    3. Connect optional servos by channel number and document the mapping.
    4. Keep motor power wiring separate from sensitive logic wiring where possible.
    5. Perform a continuity check for shorts between power and ground.
    Example channel map Channel 0 -> Left wheel Channel 1 -> Right wheel Channel 2 -> Pan servo Channel 3 -> Tilt servo
    Tip: place a paper label on each cable with the channel number. It saves a lot of time during debugging.

    6. Power and Safety

    • Check battery polarity before every first connection.
    • Confirm your battery voltage matches the motor and logic path.
    • Use an accessible external power switch.
    • Keep an emergency stop habit: wheels off ground for first tests.
    • Use the browser emergency reboot / kill-switch workflow only after master registration is confirmed.
    If the robot starts moving unexpectedly: cut main power first, then inspect wiring, servo type configuration, and startup scripts.

    7. Firmware Bring-Up

    Once construction is complete, bring the software stack up in small steps.

    1. Upload the firmware from this project.
    2. Boot the board and note the displayed token and Wi-Fi details.
    3. Open the main control page, then the script page if needed.
    4. Register as master using the token.
    5. Attach only one output first and test it at low speed.
    6. Repeat for all channels after direction and type are verified.
    Typical development flow source venv/bin/activate pio run pio run --target upload pio device monitor

    8. Validation Checklist

    • K10 boots without brownout or reset loop.
    • Display is visible and buttons are accessible.
    • Wi-Fi page loads from another device.
    • Master registration succeeds with the current token.
    • Left and right drive channels move in the expected direction.
    • Emergency reboot path is reachable from the browser.
    • No wires rub, stretch, or heat up during movement.

    9. Troubleshooting

    Bot does not power on

    Check switch wiring, battery charge, polarity, and connector seating.

    Moves backward instead of forward

    Swap motor leads or invert the channel logic in your control script.

    Wi-Fi page unreachable

    Verify the board actually joined the expected network or AP mode.

    Servo chatters or overheats

    Check power supply capacity, servo type selection, and mechanical binding.

    This guide is intentionally hardware-agnostic. If you finalize a specific chassis and wiring standard for your team, you can extend this file with exact photos, channel maps, and torque values.
